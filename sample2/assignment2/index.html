What is the Oxford English Dictionary's definition of a narrative?

As written in the article, the Oxford English Dictionary (OED) defines the word Narrative as:
"An account of a series of events, facts, etc., given in order and with the establishing of connections between them."
OED has since made the definition more concise:
Noun: A spoken or written account of connected events; a story.

What's the point of Figure 7?

The goal of the entire paper is to systematically review the design [of visualizations]. The authors select 58 visualizations from online journals, business articles and viuslazation research papers and examine them in detail. The analysis is expressed in the form of the table shown in figure 7. The table, which is described as a "design space" shows the 58 visualization, where each carries certain charactersistics which it may share with other visualizations.

Figure 7

The narrative componenets of each visualization are identified and categorized. A visualzation may contain (+) or lack (-) a feature, or the feature may not be applicable (gray).

  • One of seven genres
  • As few as zero but as many as eleven out of sixteen visual narratives which are grouped into three sub-categories
  • At least one of seventeen navarrative structures, which are also grouped into three sub-categories

Finally four red boxes show patterns which the authors have decided to highlight in the table.

  1. Narrative formats
  2. Consistency in interaction design
  3. Consistent under-utilization of “tacit tutorials” and “stimulating default views”
  4. Under-utilization of common narrative messaging techniques
With this expansive categorization this table becomes somewhat daunting and overwhelming.

Use Figure 7 to find the most common design choice within each category for the Visual narrative and Narrative structure (the categories within visual narrative are 'visual structuring', 'highlighting', etc).

Although most design features are somewhat correlated with genres, each sub-category has a most prominent feature. They are shown in the table below:

Visual Narrative / Narrative Structure Most Common Design Feature
Visual structuring Consistent visual platform
Highlighting Feature distinction
Transition guidance Object continuety
Ordering User directed path

  • What is your favorite genre of narrative visualization?

    Somewhere between an annotated charts and films lays our favorite narrative genre. Namely the slideshow. By telling a clear story chronologically, yet giving the reader the flexibility to stop, contemplate and explore along the way is a best-of-both-worlds kind of visualization.

  • What is your least favorite genre?

    Considering the tools and technologies that are available, exploring a data set with a magazine style wall of text is a choice that better have a good reason. With the average attention span dropping through the floor, we'd assume most audiences prefer content that appeals to more than just the text-proccessing part of the brain.
    In some cases - where copious background information is required - the wall of text may be the only way out.
